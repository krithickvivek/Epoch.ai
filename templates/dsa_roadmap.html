Interview Tips

Battle-tested strategies from engineers who cracked FAANG and top Indian product companies.

1
Master Patterns, Not Problems
Instead of grinding 500+ random problems, focus on the 15-20 core patterns (Two Pointers, Sliding Window, BFS/DFS, DP patterns, etc.). Once you recognize the pattern, 80% of the problem is already solved.
2
Think Out Loud
Interviewers evaluate your thought process, not just the final code. Start with brute force, discuss time/space trade-offs, then optimize. Communicate your approach before writing a single line.
3
Time-Box Your Practice
In real interviews you get 30-45 minutes per problem. Practice with a timer. If you cannot solve it in 25 minutes, read the editorial — struggling beyond that has diminishing returns.
4
Revise with Spaced Repetition
Solve a problem today, revisit it after 3 days, then after 7 days. This fights the forgetting curve and builds long-term retention. Epoch's AI agents automate this for you.
5
Company-Specific Prep
Two weeks before your interview, shift focus to that company's most-asked problems. Amazon loves BFS/DFS and DP; Google favors graph and string problems; Flipkart tests arrays and greedy heavily.
6
Handle Edge Cases First
Before coding, list edge cases: empty input, single element, duplicates, negative numbers, integer overflow. Mentioning these proactively signals maturity to your interviewer.
