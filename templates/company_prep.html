TCS National Qualifier Test (NQT)

India's largest mass recruiter. NQT is a gateway for Ninja, Digital, and Prime roles.

i Overview

TCS NQT is conducted twice a year and serves as the primary hiring assessment for TCS. The test is adaptive — your performance in early sections determines the difficulty of later questions. Scores are valid for 2 years and can be used across multiple TCS hiring drives.

Eligibility: 60% throughout (10th, 12th, UG). No active backlogs. BE/BTech/MCA/MSc graduates.

Roles via NQT:

  • Ninja — 3.36 LPA (score above sectional cutoffs)
  • Digital — 7 LPA (score in top percentile)
  • Prime — 9 LPA (invite-only, top NQT scores + CodeVita)

P Exam Pattern

Section Questions Duration Key Topics
Verbal Ability 24 30 min Reading Comprehension, Grammar, Sentence Completion, Para Jumbles
Quantitative Aptitude 26 40 min Number Systems, Algebra, Geometry, P&C, Probability, DI
Reasoning Ability 30 50 min Puzzles, Arrangements, Blood Relations, Coding-Decoding, Syllogisms
Programming Logic 10 15 min Pseudocode, Output Prediction, Basic DSA concepts
Coding (Ninja/Digital) 1-2 15 min String manipulation, Arrays, Basic Algorithms
Note: No negative marking. Each section has a separate timer. The coding section uses C / C++ / Java / Python.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Start with Verbal — it is the easiest section to score. Practice 2 RCs daily and revise grammar rules.
  • 2 For Quant, focus on Number Systems, Percentages, P&C, and DI. These repeat every year with slight variation.
  • 3 Reasoning is the heaviest section. Practice seating arrangement, puzzles, and syllogisms from previous NQT papers.
  • 4 Programming Logic requires understanding pseudocode output — practice 20-30 pseudocode problems to build pattern recognition.
  • 5 For Digital role, solve medium-level coding problems on arrays and strings. HackerRank easy-medium tier is sufficient.
  • 6 Take at least 5 full-length mock tests under timed conditions. Analyze weak areas after each mock.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

Quantitative Aptitude Number System
If the sum of two numbers is 55 and the HCF and LCM of these numbers are 5 and 120 respectively, then the sum of the reciprocals of the numbers is equal to?
Reasoning Coding-Decoding
In a certain code language, "COMPUTER" is written as "GSQTYXIV". How will "LANGUAGE" be written in that code?
Programming Logic Pseudocode
What is the output of: x = 5; y = 0; while(x > 0) { y = y + x; x = x - 1; } print(y);
I

Infosys InfyTQ / Specialist Programmer

InfyTQ certification + campus drives. Separate tracks for SP (higher CTC) and Systems Engineer.

i Overview

Infosys hires through multiple tracks: Systems Engineer (SE) at 3.6 LPA, Specialist Programmer (SP) at 6.5 LPA, and Digital Specialist Engineer (DSE) at 9.5 LPA. InfyTQ is a free certification platform — high performers get direct interview calls.

Eligibility: 60% in 10th, 12th, and UG. No standing arrears. BE/BTech from recognized universities.

Process: Online Test → InfyTQ Score → Technical Interview → HR Interview

P Exam Pattern

SectionQuestionsDurationKey Topics
Quantitative Aptitude 15 25 min Profit & Loss, Time & Work, Averages, Ratios, Number Series
Logical Reasoning 15 25 min Data Sufficiency, Arrangements, Puzzles, Sequences
Verbal Ability 20 20 min RC, Error Spotting, Sentence Completion, Vocabulary
Programming (MCQ) 10 20 min OOPs, Data Structures, DBMS, OS fundamentals
Puzzle Solving / Coding 3-5 60 min Hands-on coding (Arrays, Strings, Patterns), Puzzles for SP track
SP Track: The Specialist Programmer test has additional advanced DSA and system design MCQs. Coding round requires solving 3 problems in 60 minutes.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Complete the free InfyTQ certification courses on their platform — it directly impacts your shortlisting chances.
  • 2 Infosys Aptitude is moderate. Focus on Time-Speed-Distance, Profit-Loss, and Number Series patterns.
  • 3 For the SP track, practice medium-level coding on arrays, strings, and recursion. Python is preferred.
  • 4 Puzzle questions are unique to Infosys — practice logical puzzles involving arrangements, scheduling, and optimization.
  • 5 Revise DBMS (SQL queries, normalization) and OS (scheduling, deadlocks) for technical MCQs.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

Quantitative Number Series
Find the next number in the series: 2, 6, 14, 30, 62, ?
Logical Reasoning Puzzle
Five friends — A, B, C, D, E — sit in a row. A sits next to B but not next to C. D sits at one of the ends. E is not adjacent to D. Who sits in the middle?
Programming DBMS
Which normal form deals with the removal of transitive dependency?
W

Wipro NLTH / Elite National Talent Hunt

Mass hiring through NLTH and Project Engineer roles. Separate Elite track for higher packages.

i Overview

Wipro hires through NLTH (National Level Talent Hunt) for Project Engineer roles at 3.5 LPA, and Elite track at 6.5 LPA for top performers. The Turbo track offers up to 6.5 LPA with additional technical assessment.

Eligibility: 60% in 10th, 12th, and UG. No active backlogs at the time of joining.

Process: Online Aptitude → Written Communication → Technical Interview → HR Round

P Exam Pattern

SectionQuestionsDurationKey Topics
Aptitude (Quant + Logical) 60 60 min Quant: Percentages, Averages, DI; Logical: Series, Analogies, Blood Relations
Written Communication 1 Essay 20 min Essay on given topic (200-300 words). Grammar, coherence, and structure matter.
Technical (for Turbo/Elite) 20 20 min C/Java basics, DBMS, Networking, OS concepts
Coding (Turbo/Elite only) 2 30 min Simple-medium coding: Strings, Arrays, Sorting, Number manipulation
Important: The written communication test carries significant weight. Poor essays lead to rejection even with good aptitude scores.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Wipro Aptitude is relatively easier. Focus on speed — 60 questions in 60 minutes means 1 minute per question.
  • 2 Practice essay writing daily. Common topics: technology impact, education, environment, current affairs. Keep it structured: intro, 2-3 body paragraphs, conclusion.
  • 3 For Turbo/Elite, revise Java or C basics — inheritance, polymorphism, exception handling. These are commonly asked.
  • 4 Coding problems are easy-medium. Practice 20-30 problems from arrays and strings on any competitive coding platform.
  • 5 In the interview round, be prepared to explain your resume projects in detail. Wipro values communication skills heavily.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

Aptitude Percentage
A shopkeeper marks his goods 30% above cost price but allows a discount of 10%. Find his gain percentage.
Logical Series
Find the missing number: 3, 7, 15, 31, 63, ?
Written Essay
Write an essay on "The role of artificial intelligence in transforming education in India" (200-300 words).

Accenture Recruitment Assessment

Three-section online assessment for ASE roles. Separate advanced coding for higher packages.

i Overview

Accenture hires for Associate Software Engineer (ASE) at 4.5 LPA and Packaged App Development (PAD) roles at 6.5 LPA. The assessment has three clear sections with no negative marking.

Eligibility: 60% in 10th and 12th, 65% in UG. No active backlogs. Maximum 1 year gap in education.

Process: Online Assessment → Coding Round (for higher roles) → Communication Assessment → HR Interview

P Exam Pattern

SectionQuestionsDurationKey Topics
Cognitive (English + Aptitude + Reasoning) 50 50 min Verbal Ability, Abstract Reasoning, Numerical Ability, Letter/Number Series
Technical Assessment 40 40 min C/C++, Java, DBMS, Networking, Cloud basics, OS, Pseudocode
Coding 2 45 min DSA problems — Arrays, Strings, Sorting, Searching. One easy + one medium.
Scoring: No negative marking. Cognitive and Technical are MCQ-based. Coding is evaluated on test case pass rate and code quality.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Cognitive section is very fast-paced (1 min/question). Practice abstract reasoning and number series for speed.
  • 2 Technical section covers breadth, not depth. Revise basics of C, Java (OOPs), DBMS (SQL), and Networking (OSI layers, protocols).
  • 3 Accenture coding problems are typically easy-medium. Focus on string manipulation, array operations, and basic sorting.
  • 4 The communication assessment tests spoken English — practice clear, structured responses to common behavioral questions.
  • 5 Accenture values cloud and emerging tech knowledge. Briefly study AWS/Azure basics and AI/ML concepts for the interview.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

Cognitive Abstract
Identify the pattern: Each shape in the sequence rotates 45 degrees clockwise and alternates between filled and empty. What comes next?
Technical DBMS
Which SQL clause is used to filter records after a GROUP BY aggregation?
Coding Arrays
Given an array of integers, find the contiguous subarray with the maximum sum. Return the sum. (Kadane's Algorithm)
A

Amazon SDE Hiring

Highly competitive campus hiring for SDE-1 roles. Strong focus on DSA and Leadership Principles.

i Overview

Amazon hires SDE-1 from top colleges with packages ranging from 12-32 LPA (base + RSU + joining bonus). The hiring process is rigorous and focuses heavily on data structures, algorithms, and Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles.

Eligibility: CGPA 7.0+ (varies by college). CSE/IT branches preferred. Some drives are open to all branches.

Process: Online Assessment (OA) → Technical Interview 1 (DSA) → Technical Interview 2 (DSA + System Design) → Behavioral (LP) Round → Bar Raiser (optional)

P Exam Pattern

RoundFormatDurationKey Areas
Online Assessment (OA) 2-3 Coding + MCQs 90 min Arrays, Trees, Graphs, DP, Greedy. MCQs on CS fundamentals.
Technical Round 1 1-2 Coding + Discussion 60 min Medium-Hard DSA: Trees, Graphs, DP, Linked Lists. Explain approach clearly.
Technical Round 2 1-2 Coding + Design 60 min Hard DSA + Low-Level Design (LLD). Class design, OOPs, scalability basics.
Behavioral / LP Round STAR-format questions 45 min Amazon Leadership Principles. "Tell me about a time when..." format.
LP Focus: Customer Obsession, Ownership, Invent & Simplify, Bias for Action, Dive Deep, Deliver Results are the most frequently tested principles.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Solve 200+ LeetCode problems. Focus on: Arrays, Trees, Graphs, DP, Sliding Window, Two Pointers. Amazon repeats patterns.
  • 2 Master BFS/DFS on trees and graphs — at least 30-40 problems. Amazon loves graph traversal problems.
  • 3 Practice explaining your approach out loud while coding. Amazon interviewers evaluate communication as much as correctness.
  • 4 Prepare 8-10 STAR stories mapping to Amazon LPs. Each story should cover: Situation, Task, Action, Result with metrics.
  • 5 For the OA, solve under timed conditions. Focus on getting optimal solutions — brute force may not pass all test cases.
  • 6 Study basic system design: URL shortener, parking lot, LRU cache, notification system. Amazon tests LLD at SDE-1 level.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

OA Coding Graphs
Given a grid of 0s and 1s, find the number of distinct islands. Two islands are distinct if their shapes are different. (BFS/DFS + shape hashing)
Technical Round Trees
Given a binary tree, return the vertical order traversal of its nodes' values. Handle nodes at the same position by sorting them.
Behavioral Leadership
Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information. What was the outcome? (Tests: Bias for Action, Deliver Results)
M

Microsoft SDE / SWE Hiring

Premium campus hiring with focus on problem-solving, coding, and design thinking. Group Fly + interviews.

i Overview

Microsoft hires SDE/SWE from top IITs, NITs, and IIIT-H with packages ranging from 15-42 LPA (base + stocks). The process includes a unique "Group Fly" round — a pen-and-paper coding test in groups before interviews.

Eligibility: CGPA 7.0+ (varies by college). Open to CSE, IT, ECE, EEE branches typically.

Process: Online Assessment → Group Fly Round → Technical Interview 1 → Technical Interview 2 → AA (As Appropriate) / Hiring Manager Round

P Exam Pattern

RoundFormatDurationKey Areas
Online Assessment 3 Coding Problems 75 min Arrays, Strings, Trees, Graphs, DP. Difficulty: 1 Easy + 1 Medium + 1 Hard.
Group Fly 2-3 Problems (Pen & Paper) 60 min Write code on paper. Clean code, correct logic, and edge case handling matter.
Technical Interview 1 1-2 DSA Problems 60 min Trees, Linked Lists, Dynamic Programming. Focus on optimal solutions.
Technical Interview 2 1 DSA + Design 60 min Complex DSA + LLD. Design a data structure, implement a cache, etc.
AA / Hiring Manager Behavioral + Technical 45 min Culture fit, past projects, growth mindset, collaboration. Some DSA possible.
Group Fly: This is unique to Microsoft. You write code on paper without IDE support. Practice writing clean, syntactically correct code without autocomplete.

S Preparation Strategy

  • 1 Solve 250+ LeetCode problems. Microsoft favors Trees, Graphs, and DP heavily. Focus on medium-hard difficulty.
  • 2 For Group Fly, practice writing complete code on paper — including edge cases and proper syntax. No IDE means no autocomplete.
  • 3 Microsoft interviewers expect you to discuss multiple approaches before coding. Always start with brute force, then optimize.
  • 4 Study LLD thoroughly: design patterns (Singleton, Factory, Observer), SOLID principles, and class diagram design.
  • 5 Microsoft values "growth mindset" — be ready to discuss how you learn from failures and adapt. Prepare 3-4 such stories.
  • 6 Revise bit manipulation and math-based problems. Microsoft OA often includes one clever math/bit problem.

Q Previous Year Sample Questions

Online Assessment DP
Given a string, find the length of the longest palindromic subsequence. Return the minimum number of insertions to make the string a palindrome.
Group Fly Trees
Given a binary search tree and two nodes, find the lowest common ancestor (LCA). Write complete code on paper with edge case handling.
Technical Interview Design
Design an LRU Cache that supports get(key) and put(key, value) operations in O(1) time complexity. Discuss your choice of data structures.

R Recommended Preparation Order

If you are targeting multiple companies, this is the optimal preparation sequence to maximize overlap:

  1. Phase 1 (Months 1-2): Aptitude + Verbal + Reasoning — covers TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture. This is your safety net.
  2. Phase 2 (Months 2-3): Basic Programming + CS Fundamentals (DBMS, OS, Networking) — needed for all company tech rounds.
  3. Phase 3 (Months 3-5): DSA (Medium-Hard) — covers Amazon, Microsoft, and gives you an edge in Infosys SP / Accenture PAD.
  4. Phase 4 (Month 5+): System Design + Behavioral Prep — final layer for product companies. Start STAR stories early.
